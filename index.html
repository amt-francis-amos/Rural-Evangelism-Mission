WHO WE ARE

We are an Organization / Ministering that seeks to propagate the gospel of Jesus Christ. Our main objectives are to preach the gospel yet provide food and water for non-Christians(listeners) especially the poor members in various congregations…Matthew 15:32. Some of the pictures in the gallery present a vivid picture of what Rural Evangelism Missions did when we provided food, drinks and water for several congregations and non-Christians in the villages and towns during Covid-19 pandemic. This was made possible through the generosity of Hard Mike Hewlett of blessed memory, Harding Street Church of Christ and Roswell Church of Christ. Your donation will go a long way in preaching the gospel and providing food and water for the brethren in the hinterlands and non-believers. Donate to the course of Christ! Donate to a good course! A donation is a gift for charity, humanitarian aid or to benefit a cause. A donation may take various forms including money alms services or goods such as clothing, toys, food, or vehicles.

I was born on the 25th of September 1976. My father took me to church( Church of Christ) in 1981(at age 5) same day my father was baptized. My father attended Ghana Bible College after few years of becoming a member in the year 1986 through 1988 when he graduated. My father’s ministry began in 1990 when he established the first congregation called Central Church of Christ. As a firstborn, I readily became an integral part of my father’s ministry. Same here we received 12 American Missionaries from Alaska and Washington States. I don’t really remember all the names of those missionaries but few. I remembered Brent Dodge, Liz Dodge, little boy Bob Austin, Roy Knott, Brother Jonas(he died few years after returning to the States). That was the first time I became an interpreter to these lovely missionaries during Open and House to House evangelism. ‘Brilliant little boy Ebenezer’ suddenly became a friend to all these Missionaries and I was promised to be taken back to the States in furtherance of my education, but it didn’t materialize due to unforeseen circumstances. I was appointed an associate minister to my dad after my baptism in 1991.Since 1991,I have been working with my dad, establishing congregations all over the region until he had a stroke some 4years ago and died latter part of last year and was buried on the 30th of January 2022.

Brother Ebenezer Hayford

Missionary
